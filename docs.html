About this project

This is a proof-of-concept built by the Eric and Wendy Schmidt Center for Data Science & Environment (DSE) at UC Berkeley to explore the data behind the Barred Owl Tool inside the GLEN architecture. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or maintained by the tool's authors.

The Barred Owl Tool is a decision-support tool that prioritizes where to concentrate invasive barred owl (Strix varia) population control to protect the northern spotted owl (Strix occidentalis caurina), complementing the USFWS Barred Owl Management Strategy. All of the science, modeling (via the Zonation algorithm), and the original interactive tool are the work of its authors — full credit and authority rest with them.

Use the official tool. For authoritative results, methods, and guidance, please visit the original application and its documentation:

  • barredowltool.russell.wisc.edu — the official Barred Owl Tool (Background, Methods, How-to guide)
  • USFWS Barred Owl Management — the management strategy this tool supports

Coproduced by the University of Wisconsin–Madison Peery Wildlife Ecology + Conservation Lab and the US Fish & Wildlife Service, with support from NASA's Ecological Conservation program.

This demo mirrors the tool's outputs into cloud-native formats and pairs them with an AI assistant. Because it re-processes and re-hosts the data, values or styling here may differ from the official tool — treat this as an exploratory interface, not a source of record.

How it works

This app is powered by GLEN, DSE's open-source framework for AI-assisted geospatial exploration. Open datasets stream straight to your browser from cloud-native formats — no tile server in between — and an AI assistant answers questions by writing live queries against the same data. Technical details are in the GLEN documentation.

🌱 Low-carbon, open models. GLEN uses more environmentally-friendly AI models — faster than most humans, if not the very largest commercial models. See the carbon dashboard 🌱 for the CO₂/energy use of this deployment.

What you can do

The map shows the tool's input layers — northern spotted owl carrying capacity, pair occupancy and nest/roost habitat; barred owl occupancy; large-fire suitability; fire refugia; and federal reserve allocation — each on the native ~5 km² hex grid. The assistant can both drive the map and answer analytical questions over the Zonation priority scores for all 2,754 management scenarios (management unit × threat mode × tuning parameter × weight level). Try:

  • "Where is northern spotted owl carrying capacity highest?"
  • "For the range-wide current-threat scenario at high NSO weight, which 20 hexes rank highest?"
  • "Compare priority scores between the current-threat and future-threat scenarios."

Data & sources

Data mirror: STAC collection (public-barred-owl) — processed by the Boettiger Lab from the Barred Owl Tool. Underlying inputs include NSO pair occupancy (Lesmeister et al.), spotted-owl nest/roost habitat (Davis et al. 2022), barred-owl landscape use (Jenkins et al. 2025, J. Biogeography), USGS GAP species, large-fire suitability (Davis et al. 2017), OSU Eco-Vis fire refugia, and NWFP federal reserve allocations. See the official tool's Methods page for full provenance.
